## Key Talking Points

### Why This Matters

1. **Discovery Problem**: Library of Things programs are amazing, but discovery is fragmented. Each library has its own webpage, making it hard for patrons to find what's available.

2. **Equity**: These programs provide free access to tools and equipment that many people can't afford to buy, especially for one-time use.

3. **Sustainability**: Sharing resources reduces consumption and waste.

### Notable Findings

1. **Morse Institute Library** (Natick) has the most extensive tool collection in Massachusetts:
   - 168+ verified items
   - Actual power tools (20V drill, pressure washer)
   - Complete home inspection equipment
   - Far surpasses any CWMARS library

2. **Minuteman Network** is ahead of CWMARS in Library of Things adoption:
   - 12,000+ items across 40+ libraries
   - Better online documentation
   - More diverse collections

3. **CWMARS** has fewer documented programs but larger geographic coverage.
